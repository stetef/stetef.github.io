    The following books I have read (or listened to) in 2023. I have short blurbs below about each. Of all the books I read this year, I would recommend To Shape a Dragon's Breath by Moniquill Blackgoose.

    Hopefully coming soon as well is a form where you can share with me any books you think I should read!

    Spinning Silver by Naomi Novik
    - A retelling of Rumpelstiltskin but totally cool because it's Naomi Novik

    The City We Became my N.K. Jemisin
    - I liked the Killing Moon series better but this was fun, esp if you are familiar with the NYC boroughs

    Burn by Patrick Ness
    - Cold War meets dragons and parallel universes. I liked the weird religious dragon cult lol

    A Lady's Guide to Celestial Mechanics by Olivia Waite
    - A feminist, spicy romance book about science and art

    Come As You Are by Emily Nagoski
    - Would recommend for anyone who is a woman, or loves them

    The Witch's Heart by Genevieve Gornichec
    - I suppose this retelling of Angrbo√∞a is better than traditional Nose mythology but it still made me cringe

    Wool by Hugh Howey
    - Dystopian, don't trust anyone, and watch as the puzzle unfurls with these badass female characters

    Deadly Education, The Last Graduate, The Golden Enclaves by Naomi Novik
    - I LOVE NAOMI NOVIK. This high stakes magic school (you actually feel like the stakes are high) with a main character like Wednesday Addams

    Lessons in Chemistry by Bonnie Garmus
    - The first half of this book triggered me and then I was just sad

    The Amulet of Samarkand by Jonathan Stroud
    - The use of footnotes and the humor is amazing

    Ninth House, Hell Bent by Leigh Bardugo
    - Yale secret societies actually do dark magic. Love talking to my friends who went to Yale about this hehe

    Polysecure by Jessica Fern
    - An interesting perspective on attachment theory

    The Priory of the Orange Tree by Samantha Shannon
    - Wow so much to say here. Loved the strong female cast but honestly I would have just read a story about Ead

    Our Missing Hearts by Celeste Ng
    - Just a good book all around 10/10

    And Finally by Henry Marsh
    - Did not like this at all ugh would NOT recommend. It just felt like an old dude complaining about being old (not insightful), virtue signaling, and blaming testosterone on his infidelty and failed first marriage (my dude what)

    All Systems Red by Martha Wells
    - Fun satirical quick read about a cyborg programmed to kill things that hacked its brain so that it could watch soap operas in its spare time

    What If? by Randall Munroe
    - Fun lil read

    Daughter of the Moon Goddess by Sue Lynn Tan
    - The plot was a little predictable but still a good read

    An Ember in the Ashes, A Torch Against the Night, A Reaper at the Gates, A Sky Beyond the Storm by Sabaa Tahir
    - I fell in love with this series so much wow just check it out it is amazing. My only negative opinion of the entire series is that parts of the romance between Laia and Elias are pretty cringey with sterotypes

    Tomorrow, and Tomorrow, and Tomorrow by Gabrielle Zevin
    - So good. I'd say this is a story about relationships of all kinds with really clever nonchronological excerpts. Will make you feel things

    Fourth Wing, Iron Flame by Rebecca Yarros
    - Book 1 was entertaining and hella spicy even though it was hella tropey. I actually liked the unique meshing of romance and fantasy -- the world always felt accessible unlike a lot of other fantasy books that drop you in the middle of fantasy lingo and you are confused the first quarter of the book. The tropes just meant you can't take it too seriously. Book 2 went downhill, you don't need to read it

    The Butcher and the Wren by Alaina Urquhart
    - Interesting POV from a psychopathic killer. Had to reread the plot twist to understand what was happening though

    All That She Carried by Tiya Alicia Miles
    - About Ashley's sack, and what it takes to put together its historical story, especially when information is missing or incomplete

    The House in the Cerulean Sea by TJ Klune
    - A good wholesome book with characters that all feel different yet relatable in the best possible away

    A Darker Shade of Magic, A Gathering of Shadows, A Conjuring of Light by V. E. Schwab
    - The audiobook narrators change in books 2 and 3 and make it infintely better. I loved the worlds and the characters in this. Mostly, I love V.E. Schwab's work

    Gideon the Ninth, Harrow the Ninth by Tamsyn Muir
    - Don't get me wrong, the books are good. I love Gideon. And maybe listening to this as an audiobook made it worse, but I was (and still am) very confused, although I have a feeling that was Tamsyn Muir's intention

    The Invisible Life of Addie LaRue by V.E. Schwab
    - V.E. Schwab, is this you admitting to selling your soul to write such good books? (Yes, the meta commentary in this was hilarious.) The nonchronological storytelling was really well done

    Storm Front by Jim Butcher
    - I would describe this as popcorn to read on stormy fall evenings. Clearly written by a dude though lol

    Twisted Love, Twisted Games by Ana Huang
    - Smutty BDSM, what more do you want

    The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo by Taylor Jenkins Reid
    - Really interesting. Again the parallel stories told in chronological order was well done and I did NOT catch the twist at the end

    The Stardust Thief by Chelsea Abdullah
    - I liked the City of Brass series by S. A. Chakraborty a little better, but it was really interesting to compare the different takes on similar themes between the two series (e.g. the magical significance of rings and the different social classes of djinn). Aisha takes the crown though

    To Shape a Dragon's Breath by Moniquill Blackgoose
    - This might be my new favorite dragon book. I especially like how the cadence feels like an oral story, and oral stories are prevelant in the story itself. Can't wait for book 2! Of all the books I read this year, I would recommend reading this one!

    Shadow and Bone by Leigh Bardugo
    - I have to admit that I saw the Netflix show before reading this... The Crows were my favorite part of the show though so it was a little sad not to see them here

    Chemistry by Weike Wang
    - I can see how this might trigger grad students but the interludes and very dry/dark sense of humor made it hilarious and any grad student will relate

    A Court of Thorns and Roses by Sarah J. Maas
    - I have been procrastinating on reading these because it was SO mainstream but I was promised it actually breaks the tropes it sets up so I was just waiting for the shoe to drop... Apparently book 2 is where it is at though...
