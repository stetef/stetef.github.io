  • I am not crazy

    This poem is my take on rejecting tradition femininity. I'm sure other people with more education or experience in this field will have better things to say, but I just tried to portray my own feelings here.

    Content warning: Explicit language

    You call me unladylike.

    My laughs are loud, often accompanied by a snort. I fart and burp, and I don’t apologize. I talk openly about my menstrual cycle and therapy. I enjoy getting sweaty and dirty. I hate dresses and heels and makeup. I like it when my friends tell me I’m pretty. I have many guy friends who I don’t want to have sex with even though I enjoy sex with men.

    You call me a bitch.

    I speak my mind. I call people out on their shit. I throw things at the wall when I am mad. Sometimes, I randomly shout FUCK SHIT BALLS. I fantasize about beating the shit out of someone, just to imagine what physical power is like. Like that dude on the street who called me “beautiful” when all he saw was an object to conquer.

    You call me a slut.

    I stalk crushes on social media using one of my many fake accounts. I am horny all the time and enjoy hooking up. I am not opposed to sex on a first date. I have participated in two orgies. I own a closet full of sex toys and paraphernalia. My friends call it my sex dungeon even though I have not tried BDSM. I want to get into it though.

    You call me crazy.

    I take sick days when I don’t want to work for “mental health.” I take prescriptions for my anxiety and depression. I am a woman who thinks babies are NOT cute and pregnancy is GROSS. The idea of marriage makes me want to RUN.

    I AM NOT CRAZY.

    My body is me. I don’t fear my emotions. I know how to satisfy my desires. I am normal. I AM FREE.

    You are the crazy one.

    Fuck your rules.
